Development and Testing of an Online Oil Condition Monitor for Diesel Driven Army Ground Vehicles
SAE World Congress
16 April 2012
http://papers.sae.org/2012-01-1348/

Describes the development and testing of an online oil condition monitor that utilizes a multi-frequency approach to electrochemical impedance spectroscopy to interrogate and evaluate fluid health in near real time. A dual microcontroller processing architecture embedded in the sensor itself executes an oil-health evaluation algorithm and provides estimates of lubricant remaining useful life, as well as identification of the primary mode of degradation of the fluid.

American Helicopter Society
03 March 2011
https://vtol.org/store/product/torque-sensor-validation-and-signal-recovery-for-turboshaft-engines-5282.cfm

The development of advanced algorithms designed to validate and synthesizing torque sensor readings for aircraft turboshaft engines. This solution consists of several modules, preprocessing for the reduction of noise and operational mode detection, diagnostic feature extraction, torque signal recovery, and a fault detection and classification module.
